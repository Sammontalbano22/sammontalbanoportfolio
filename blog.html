A SMASHING SUCCESS
In 2023, the gaming market hit $184 billion, dominating the entertainment industry. With cloud streaming, indie game studios, and AAA titles on the rise, gaming is not just play—it's economic power.

Gaming Eras
From the arcade machines of the '70s to today's open-world blockbusters, the industry evolved through 7 major eras—highlighting how tech and consumer behavior shape what's next.

Labor & Culture
Behind the screens are 268,000 U.S. workers. While the pay is competitive, many face long hours, diversity gaps, and the infamous "crunch culture" that plagues the industry.

Market Power & Ethics
With Microsoft, Sony, and Nintendo controlling 100% of the console market, concerns rise over monopoly and control. The government's laissez-faire stance adds fuel to the debate over regulation.

Conclusion
The gaming industry is a capitalist juggernaut—efficient, innovative, but not without its ethical and labor issues. As technology and audiences evolve, so will the challenges and opportunities.
